## Application Process
The application process for Returning students is listed below. If you have any questions about one of the steps, an Admissions Counselor is only a phone call or email away to assist you at any time in your journey!
1. Go to [**ApplyTexas**](https://www.applytexas.org) to create a profile and select the **Readmit Undergraduate** application to begin the process.
2. Prior to submitting the application, select “ _fee waiver requested_ ” to process the application for free.
_*Fee waivers are only accepted under the Readmit Undergraduate application._
3. Submit official transcripts if you meet either of the following circumstances:
(1) if you have taken college work at another institution since your last enrollment term with A&M-San Antonio
-OR-
(2) if your previously submitted transcripts are older than 5 years. 
**Official transcripts must be submitted directly from the college/university.**
The university can only accept transfer credits from regionally accredited colleges and universities. 
You may submit transcripts via one of the following methods:
  * Email to admissions@tamusa.edu
  * EDI-Electronic Data Interchange (for Texas Colleges and Universities only)


By mail to:
Texas A&M University-San Antonio
Office of Admissions
One University Way
San Antonio, TX 78224
4. Students who were previously placed on _academic dismissal or enforced withdrawal_ , must also complete the [ **academic reinstatement appeal process** ](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/academic-success-center/academic-policies/academic-reinstatement-appeal-form.html) to proceed with readmission. Please contact the Office of Admissions with any questions regarding this portion of the application process.
**[Academic Reinstatement Appeal Checklist](https://www.tamusa.edu/admissions/documents/Academic-Reinstatement-Appeal-Checklist.pdf) **

5. Complete the application for financial aid (FAFSA/TASFA). Although this is optional, many scholarships use this as a resource for eligibility. It can be completed prior to readmission to the university. 
_ *Students on academic dismissal should contact the Office of Financial Aid for information on the Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) Policy*  _

## Frequently Asked Questions
##  Can I apply to return for any semester? 
Yes. Returning students are eligible to apply for any semester. Students needing to submit a reinstatement appeal should verify both the application and appeal deadlines have not passed before applying. 
##  Do I have to submit my official transcripts even if my last school attended with A&M SA? 
Students must submit official transcripts _only_ if their previous transcripts are older than 5 years OR they have attended another college/university since their time at A&M-San Antonio. You may contact the Office of Admissions for questions regarding transcript requirements. 
##  What is the timeline to get a decision? 
The timeline to receive an admission decision will vary depending on each student’s application requirements. Admission files are not reviewed until ALL required documents have been received. Students required to submit a _reinstatement appeal_ will not receive a decision until _after_ the appeal deadline (which differs from the application deadline). 
##  How do I know if I need to submit a reinstatement appeal? 
If you are unsure of your last academic standing, please contact the Office of Admissions to connect with our Returning Student Coordinator for guidance on appeal requirements. 
##  How can I see the coursework that I already completed at A&M SA? 
If you still have access to JagApp, then you may review _Degree Works_ to see your prior courses and degree plan. Locate the _Students_ tab, then select _Classes and Registration_ and you will see Degree Works listed under the _Advising Tools_ box. 
You may contact the ITS Help Desk at (210) 784-1457 for questions about JagApp access. 
##  When can I meet with my academic advisor? 
All returning students will have a requirement (hold) to meet with an academic advisor once readmitted. If it’s been five or more years since you last attended, we recommend you meet with an advisor _before_ reapplying. Academic Advising can be contacted at (210) 784-1307.
##  Does my student ID stay the same? 
Yes. Your J or K number will remain the same. Please contact the Enrollment Services Center at (210) 784-1300 for help retrieving this information.
##  Can returning students qualify for the Jaguar Promise? 
Jaguar Promise is available to new incoming first year and transfer students that meet the criteria of the program. Returning students are not eligible.
##  How do I pay for college? And what resources do you offer at A&M-SA? 
A&M-San Antonio has a Jaguar Excellence Guarantee to provide boundless opportunities to help you achieve your dreams. For all your paying for college needs, visit the Office of Financial Aid and Scholarships office to deep dive into the funding available.
##  How can I visit campus? 
We can't wait to see you back on campus! Visit the [Campus Events](https://www.tamusa.edu/admissions/visit-campus/index.html) page to schedule a daily tour or RSVP for one of our special events.
