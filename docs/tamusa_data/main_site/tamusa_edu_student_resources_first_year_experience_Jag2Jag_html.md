About the Program 
Texas A&M University-San Antonio (A&M-SA) has partnered with Mentor Collective to connect new Jaguars with current students, creating a supportive community that fosters a strong sense of belonging during their transition to university life. Through this mentorship program, we aim to provide incoming students with the opportunity to ask questions, explore campus resources, and gain valuable insights into student life and the wide range of programs offered at TAMUSA. Our goal is to ensure that every new Jaguar feels supported, informed, and empowered to succeed during their academic journey.
![Jag X student crowd walking through the Central Academic Building Doors. ](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/first-year-experience/images/Jag2Jag_Image_Folder/Jag2Jag_About_Program_Image.png)
* * *
## Mentors & Mentees
### About The Mentors
  * Mentors are current A&M-SA students who are dedicated to supporting new Jaguars. They are upperclassmen actively working toward their degrees and eager to help guide new students through their university experience. 


  * Mentors share their knowledge, provide advice, and offer valuable insights to help new students navigate their academic, social, and professional journey at A&M-SA.


### About the Mentees
  * Mentees are new students at A&M-SA who wish to connect with a Jag2Jag Mentor to ease their transition into university life. 


  * Mentees can learn about campus resources, explore student life, and gain guidance from an experienced upperclassman. 


* * *
## What Mentors and Mentees Discuss
  * How will my major influence my career options?


  * Strategies for balancing academics, responsibilities, and social life.


  * Tips for setting myself up for success in academics, campus involvement, and career preparation.


  * How to connect with peers and make the most of campus life.


  * Accessing and utilizing A&M-SA resources effectively.


* * *
How Are Mentors And Mentees Paired?
Through the matching process, mentees are paired with mentors based on common interests, majors, and other factors to ensure a meaningful connection. Mentorship can take place via text, phone, or in-person meetings, offering flexibility and support that fits your schedule.

I became a mentor because I have lots of experience in my current role as a Peer Leader on campus. I have a passion for connecting with people and helping their college experience run more smoothly. ##  Why Mentorship Is Important 
Mentorship is more than a one-time connection—it’s a culture that transforms the student experience. Research shows that strong mentorship fosters belonging, improves academic success, and prepares students for future careers.
