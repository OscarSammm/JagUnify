## Mission
Military Affairs assists military-connected students at Texas A&M University-San Antonio so they can succeed professionally by embracing their strengths.
![Student Worker Tabling](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/military-affairs/images/Student_Worker_Tabling.png)
## Vision
Our military-connected graduates leave A&M-San Antonio with meaningful opportunities to further their career success and service to their community.
![Students wearing R.E.D Shirts](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/military-affairs/images/R.E.D_Shirts.png)

## On-Campus Resources
**SVA**
[Student Veterans of America](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/military-affairs/SVA/index-sva.html) at Texas A&M-San Antonio aims to elevate our heroes by connecting student veterans and those with a desire to support them in a community of like-minded individuals.
**ROTC**
[ROTC](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/military-affairs/rotc-jaguar-battalion.html) prepares college students to succeed in any competitive environment. The leadership training and experiences you have in the ROTC will give you a foundation to become commissioned officers upon graduation.
**Bexar County**
[Bexar County](https://www.bexar.org/509/Department-of-Military-and-Veterans-Serv) empowers Veterans, transitioning military, their families, and the military-affiliated community by providing services, connecting them to resources, and facilitating opportunities that benefit them and the community.
**TVC Health Care Advocacy  
**
[TVC Health Care Advocacy](https://tvc.texas.gov/health-care-advocacy/) acts as a liaison between veterans and the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to resolve access issues related to VA health care services.
**Disability Support Services (DSS)  
**
[Disability Support Services (DSS)](https://www.tamusa.edu/Disability-Support-Services/index.html) at Texas A&M University-San Antonio is committed to ensuring equal access, full participation, and reasonable accommodations by coordinating services that meet the unique needs of enrolled students with documented disabilities. DSS works with students as they actively participate in their academic pursuits.
**Parking**
[Parking & Transportation Services](https://resources.tamusa.edu/employees/business-affairs/parking-transportation/index.html) office aims to provide quality parking services and develop innovative transportation options. Our vision is to afford our University community with the most value through our focus on customer service, communication, adaptability, and support.  
  
Texas A&M-San Antonio makes every effort to be a Military Embracing campus. To help us reach our goal, we offer all of our disabled and decorated veterans exemption from parking permit fees. To learn more about the exemption, visit the [Disabled and Decorated Veterans page](https://resources.tamusa.edu/employees/business-affairs/parking-transportation/disabled-and-decorated-veterans.html).   

**Student Counseling Center**
[Student Counseling Center (SCC)](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/support/student-counseling-center/index.html) is a welcoming, supportive, and confidential space. Our licensed and professionally trained staff offers free and confidential mental health services to currently enrolled Texas A&M University-San Antonio students in various formats: one-on-one sessions, group therapy, and walk-in services for urgent situations. Because student fees cover these services,  _visits do not require_ out-of-pocket expenses.
**Tutoring**
[Academic Learning Center](https://www.tamusa.edu/student-resources/Academic-Success-Center/tutoring-services/index.html) is here to support you with free tutoring services. Whether you prefer in-person appointments, need quick help through walk-in math tutoring, or want to meet with an online tutor, we’ve got you covered!
